A portable shelter for any weather




Yurt alpha
The model

Before embarking on a full size build and purchasing the materials, I built a scale prototype in order to test the mechanics and geometrics of the design.

The prototype was made with some BBQ skewers, cotton thread and masking tape.

Build time: One day. Cost: £2.

With no drawings, this was used as the working design for the end product.

Scale: 1:7.3.

Yurt beta
The real thing. Version 1.0

The main frame was built from 2.4m lengths of bamboo cane cut down to 2.2m in order to fit comfortably in a Honda Jazz. The canes came in a variety of thicknesses and tapers and so the thinner, lighter canes were used for the roof,

Additional roof supports were added so that all 16 apexes carried one, rather than the 8 in the prototype.

Cable ties were used to make the joins, with polycord and gaffer tape used at the centre of the roof due to the number of members at that node. Where the drilled holes split, PVA was dabbed with gaffer tape wrapped tightly around.

A rope around the top of the wall keeps the self-weight from pushing out and provides a hanging point for the cotton twill walls. The roof is made from a heavy duty PVC waterproof liner, cut and taped into a cone shape. The groundsheet is secured to 40cm above ground to handle waterlogged ground.

Shoulder straps were added to the frame so that it can be easily carried by one person. Here is the entire kit: frame, floor, walls and roof.

Build time: One day. Cost: £80.
